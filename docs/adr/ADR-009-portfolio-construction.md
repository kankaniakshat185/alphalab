# ADR 009: Cross-Sectional Z-Score Portfolio Construction

## Status
Accepted

## Context
Phase 3 requires a strategy to convert raw factor signals (e.g., outputs from `Momentum(20)`) into a target portfolio weight allocation for backtesting. 
Different quantitative factors output signals on completely different scales. For instance, `Momentum` outputs percentage changes (e.g., `0.15`), while `Volatility` outputs standard deviations, and a custom factor could output arbitrary unbounded integers.

We needed a standardized method to normalize these cross-sectional signals into portfolio weights that are dollar-neutral and scaled consistently to a gross exposure of `1.0`.

## Options Considered

1. **Quantile / Rank-Based Allocation:** Rank all assets daily. Go long the top 10% (equal weight) and short the bottom 10% (equal weight). Ignore the middle 80%.
   - *Pros:* Highly robust to outliers. Simple to understand.
   - *Cons:* Ignores the magnitude of the signal. A top decile stock with a massive signal gets the same weight as a top decile stock with a marginal signal.

2. **Cross-Sectional Z-Scoring (Chosen):** At each time step (date), compute the mean and standard deviation of all signals across the universe. For each asset, subtract the mean and divide by the standard deviation. Finally, divide by the sum of absolute Z-scores to normalize total gross exposure to 1.0.
   - *Pros:* Accounts for signal magnitude. Results in a mathematically sound dollar-neutral portfolio (sum of weights = 0). It is the industry standard baseline for statistical arbitrage.
   - *Cons:* Sensitive to extreme outliers compared to rank-based methods.

## Decision
We will implement **Cross-Sectional Z-Scoring** as the default baseline portfolio construction methodology in `src/alphalab/engine/portfolio.py`.

## Consequences
- **Dollar Neutrality:** Portfolios generated by the engine will always be dollar-neutral by default, isolating the alpha of the factor from broader market drift (beta).
- **Handling Missing Data:** If only one asset has a valid signal on a given day (standard deviation is undefined), or all signals are identical (standard deviation is 0), the portfolio constructor will gracefully assign `0.0` weight to avoid division by zero.
- **Future Extensibility:** In future phases, we may introduce a strategy configuration object allowing the user to select between Z-Score, Rank-based, or Long-Only portfolio constructors.
