The economic ideas behind AlphaLab
No PhD required — here's what the jargon actually means.
📈

What is an alpha factor?

A mathematical signal — like momentum or volatility — that attempts to predict which stocks will outperform. If Momentum(20) is high for a stock, we bet it continues rising. The question is: is that signal real, or lucky noise?

⚖️

Sharpe Ratio

Return per unit of risk. A Sharpe of 1.0+ is solid, 2.0+ is exceptional. But a high Sharpe on historical data alone proves nothing — it might just be overfitting to that specific period.

🎯

Information Coefficient (IC)

Correlation between the factor's predicted ranking and the actual return ranking. An IC of 0.05 is considered good in practice — markets are hard to predict at any IC above 0 consistently.

📉

Max Drawdown

The worst peak-to-trough loss the factor-based portfolio suffered. A drawdown of -20% means investors lost ₹20 for every ₹100 at peak before recovery began. Lower is always better.

🔀

Robustness Score

AlphaLab's core output. We inject artificial noise (± 2% price perturbation) and drop data (up to 20% of bars). If the factor's Sharpe survives, it's robust. Score ≥ 0.8 = production ready.

📊

Look-ahead Bias

Using future data to evaluate a past signal — a subtle but fatal mistake. AlphaLab enforces point-in-time NIFTY 50 universe construction so factors only see data that existed on that date.
